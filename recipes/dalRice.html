Dal

Description

Dal Rice is a quintessential Indian dish that combines cooked lentils (dal) with steamed rice. It's wholesome, nutritious, and bursting with earthy flavors, often served with ghee (clarified butter) and pickles or papad on the side. Perfect as a comfort meal or a nutritious staple for everyday dining.

Ingredients

For the dal
  • 1/2 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas) or moong dal (yellow lentils)
  • 2 cups water (for cooking dal)
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 1 medium tomato, finely chopped
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 2 green chilies, slit (adjust to taste)
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1/2 tsp mustard seeds
  • 1/2 tsp red chili powder (optional)
  • 1/2 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 1-2 dried red chilies
  • 8-10 curry leaves
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • Salt, to taste
  • Lemon wedges (optional)
For the Rice
  • 1 cup basmati or regular rice
  • 2 cups water
  • 1/2 tsp salt (optional)

Recipe

  1. Cook the Dal
    1. Wash and Soak: Rinse the dal thoroughly and soak it in water for 15-20 minutes (optional but recommended).
    2. Pressure Cook: Add the dal, 2 cups of water, turmeric powder, and a pinch of salt to a pressure cooker. Cook for 2-3 whistles on medium heat or until soft and mushy. Alternatively, cook in a pot until tender.
    3. Mash and Set Aside: Lightly mash the cooked dal using a ladle and set aside.
  2. Prepare the Tadka (Tempering)
    1. Heat ghee or oil in a pan.
    2. Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds; let them splutter.
    3. Toss in dried red chilies, curry leaves, and green chilies. Sauté for a few seconds.
    4. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
    5. >Stir in minced garlic and grated ginger. Cook until aromatic.
    6. Add tomatoes, red chili powder, and a pinch of salt. Cook until the tomatoes soften and the oil separates.
  3. Combine Dal and Tadka
    1. Pour the cooked dal into the pan with the tadka.
    2. Mix well and add water to adjust the consistency. Bring it to a gentle boil.
    3. Simmer for 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
    4. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  4. Cook the Rice
    1. Rinse the rice thoroughly and soak it in water for 15 minutes. Drain.
    2. In a pot, add the rice, 2 cups of water, and salt (>optional). Bring to a boil.
    3. Reduce the heat, cover, and simmer until the water is absorbed and the rice is fluffy (about 10-12 minutes).
  5. Serve

Serve a portion of rice with dal ladled generously on top. Add a dollop of ghee for extra flavor. Pair with papad, pickle, or yogurt for a complete meal.
