Pav Bhaji

Description

Pav Bhaji is a popular street food from Mumbai, India, known for its rich, buttery, and spicy vegetable mash served with soft, toasted buns (pav). It's a quick, delicious, and wholesome dish perfect for parties, snacks, or a satisfying meal. The vibrant flavors of the bhaji (vegetable curry) paired with the buttery pav make it an irresistible treat.

Ingrdients

For the Bhaji
  • 3 medium potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 cup cauliflower, finely chopped
  • 1 cup green peas
  • 1 medium capsicum (bell pepper), finely chopped
  • 2 medium onions, finely chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 2 green chilies, finely chopped (optional)
  • 2 tbsp pav bhaji masala
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • ½ tsp red chili powder
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2 tbsp butter (plus more for serving)
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • ½ lemon (for garnish)
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
For the Pav
  • 8 pav buns
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • ½ tsp pav bhaji masala (optional)
Optional Sides
  • Chopped onions
  • Lemon wedges
  • Fresh coriander leaves

Steps

  1. Prepare the Vegetables
    1. Boil the potatoes, cauliflower, and green peas in a pressure cooker or pot until soft. Mash them coarsely and set aside.
  2. Cook the Bhaji
    1. Heat oil and 1 tbsp butter in a large pan or skillet.
    2. Add minced garlic, grated ginger, and green chilies. Sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.
    3. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
    4. Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and pav bhaji masala. Cook until the tomatoes soften and the oil separates.
    5. Add the mashed vegetables to the pan. Mix well and mash further with a potato masher.
    6. Adjust the consistency by adding water (about 1-1.5 cups). Simmer for 10-12 minutes, stirring occasionally.
    7. Add salt to taste and 1 tbsp butter. Mix well.
    8. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and a squeeze of lemon juice.
  3. Toast the Pav
    1. Slice the pav buns horizontally.
    2. Heat a flat griddle or tawa and melt some butter on it.
    3. Optionally, sprinkle pav bhaji masala on the butter.
    4. Place the buns on the griddle and toast until golden brown.
  4. Serve

Serve the hot bhaji in a bowl with a generous dollop of butter on top. Arrange the toasted pav buns on the side. Accompany with chopped onions, lemon wedges, and fresh coriander leaves.
