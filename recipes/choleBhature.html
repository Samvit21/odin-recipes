Chole Bhature

Chole Bhature is a beloved dish from North India, featuring spicy, tangy chickpea curry (chole) paired with deep-fried, fluffy bread (bhature). This hearty and flavorful dish is perfect for a special breakfast, lunch, or dinner, and it's guaranteed to be a crowd-pleaser.

Ingredients

For the Chole
  • 1 cup dried chickpeas (or 2 cups canned chickpeas)
  • 1 large onion, finely chopped
  • 2 medium tomatoes, pureed
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch ginger, grated
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 2 tsp chole masala
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • ½ tsp red chili powder
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • ½ tsp amchur (dry mango powder) or juice of half a lemon
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped (for garnish)
  • Salt, to taste
For the Bhature
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour (maida)
  • 2 tbsp semolina (rava)
  • ¼ cup yogurt
  • ½ tsp sugar
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp baking soda
  • Water, as needed (to knead the dough)
  • Oil, for deep frying

Steps

  1. Prepare the Chickpeas
    1. If using dried chickpeas, soak them overnight in water. Drain and pressure cook with water and a pinch of salt for 4-5 whistles until tender. Drain and set aside. (Skip this step if using canned chickpeas; rinse and set aside.)
  2. Cook the Chole
    1. Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
    2. Add minced garlic, grated ginger, and green chilies. Sauté until aromatic.
    3. Add chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.
    4. Stir in tomato puree, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and chole masala. Cook until the oil separates.
    5. Add the cooked chickpeas and mix well. Mash a few chickpeas for a thicker gravy.
    6. Add water to adjust the consistency and simmer for 10-12 minutes.
    7. Stir in garam masala and amchur powder (or lemon juice). Garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
  3. Prepare the Bhature Dough
    1. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them into 6-8-inch circles.
    2. Heat oil in a deep pan or kadai. Fry the rolled bhature one at a time until they puff up and turn golden.
    3. Drain on paper towels to remove excess oil.
  4. Serve

Serve the hot chole in a bowl with freshly fried bhature on the side. Pair with sliced onions, green chilies, and lemon wedges for a complete experience.
